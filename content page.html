Covid-19 Facts

  1. Covid-19 dies fast when exposed to the UV light of sunlight.
  2. Antibiotics don't work against Covid-19 because it is a virus while antibiotics only work with bacteria infections.
  3. Symptoms of Covid-19 starts from 5-6 days after the exposure.
  4. Isolation and Quarantine are not the same thing. Isolation means that you are isolated from other people because they have the virus, while quarantine means that you are isolated from other people because you have close contact with someone with Covid-19.
  5. Covid-19 can live on surfaces for days, but sometimes it depends on the object






Types of Covid-19 vaccines (vaccines authorized in the United States)

  1. Pfizer-Biontech - This vaccine can be given to people 12 years and older. It is composed of two shots and they are given with 3 weeks or 21 days apart from each other.
  2. Moderna - This vaccine can be given to people 18 years and older. It is composed of two shots and they are given with 4 weeks or 28 days apart from each other.
  3. Johnson & Johnson’s Janssen - This vaccine can be given to people 18 years and older. It is composed of only one shot.


Covid-19 Symptoms

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Headache
  • New loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea


Conclusion:

We can see that it is tough to get affected by the Covid-19 virus. It has many symptoms which will make us feel bad. The most common symptoms of Covid-19 are fever, tiredness, loss of taste and smell, back pain, headache and many more. It also has long-term effects like long-term breathing problems, heart complications, chronic kidney impairment, stroke and Guillain-Barre syndrome that is why we need to help each other, including ourselves. How can we do that? First is by following the health and safety protocols like maintaining social distancing, wearing a mask, cleaning your hands with water and soap, practicing cough etiquette. Second is to get vaccinated. We can always be sure that we won’t get severe cases when we’re vaccinated. In this way, we can also make other people that are around us be safe.
